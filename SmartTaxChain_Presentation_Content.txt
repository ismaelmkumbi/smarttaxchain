SLIDE 3: CURRENT TAX SYSTEM CHALLENGES
================================================================================

Title: Current Tax System Challenges
Subtitle: Three critical issues plaguing Tanzania's tax system

─────────────────────────────────────
CHALLENGE 1: TAX EVASION
─────────────────────────────────────
Kicker: Evidence from national briefing

Key Stats:
• Tanzania loses 1.3% of GDP annually
• Approximately USD 1 Billion (TZS 3 Trillion)

Lead:
Revenue leakage is driven by non-issuance of receipts, under-reporting, and 
record manipulation—creating an unfair burden on compliant taxpayers.

Bullet Points:
• Common methods: failure to issue receipts, under-reporting sales, manipulation of records
• Informal activity + hidden income reduces visibility and enforcement strength
• Tax-to-GDP gap signals leakage (13.7% vs SSA average 15%)
• Impact: reduced collections constrain funding for public services and infrastructure

Callout Quote:
"Tax evasion in Tanzania accounts for 1.3 percent of the country's GDP."
(The Citizen, Aug 22, 2025)

─────────────────────────────────────
CHALLENGE 2: SYSTEM FRAUD
─────────────────────────────────────
Kicker: External + internal manipulation

Key Stats:
• External attacks via Zappers
• EFD Transaction manipulation
• Africa loses USD 50–60 Billion annually

Lead:
Fraud succeeds when sales can be suppressed and records can be edited without 
immediate visibility—causing delayed discovery and weak proof.

Bullet Points:
• External: "Zappers" suppress sales and bypass fiscal controls
• Internal: edits to liabilities/penalties create silent leakage
• Pain point: investigation becomes slow and disputable because evidence is 
  incomplete or can be modified

Fraud Attack Flow:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ EXTERNAL THREAT (Zapper)                                        │
│ Entry point → Manipulation → Result                             │
│ • Suppression tool installed at POS/EFD layer                   │
│ • Receipts altered / transactions removed or reduced            │
│ • Under-reporting + delayed detection during audits             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ INTERNAL THREAT (Privilege Abuse)                               │
│ Privileged access → Silent change → Result                      │
│ • Back-office roles can edit taxpayer records                   │
│ • Liabilities reduced / penalties changed or deleted            │
│ • Hard to prove the "who/when/what changed" later               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

─────────────────────────────────────
CHALLENGE 3: AUDIT & COMPLIANCE DELAYS
─────────────────────────────────────
Kicker: Slow cycles = wider fraud window

Key Stats:
• Audit cycles: 30–180 days
• Delayed detection = delayed recovery
• Manual cross-checking reduces transparency

Lead:
Long manual verification cycles delay recovery, widen the fraud window, and 
reduce real-time control and confidence.

Bullet Points:
• Audit cycles often take 30–180 days (verification is late)
• Delayed detection causes delayed recovery — billions remain uncollected
• Manual cross-checking slows decisions and increases disputes and rework
